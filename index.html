Purpose

Ægis exists to support attention as a moral and pedagogical act. It notices when learning falters, responds with care, protects the dignity of the pupil, and refuses coercion, ranking, or extraction.

It does not monitor compliance. It does not optimize productivity. It does not label emotions, grade attention, or surveil classrooms.

What Ægis Is

Ægis Is

  • an instrument
  • a studio aid
  • a listening device for learning rhythms
  • a guardian of pace

Ægis Is Not

  • emotion recognition software
  • behavior scoring
  • affective surveillance
  • classroom analytics for authority

Presence

A subtle pulse, not a gauge. No meters. No scores. No heatmaps. No red/green.

steady
wavering
fading

How Ægis Speaks

  • “We may have moved too quickly.”
  • “Would another form help?”
  • “Shall we pause briefly?”
  • “A smaller step might be kinder.”

Ægis responds only by offering. Never by forcing, interrupting aggressively, escalating authority, or notifying third parties.

Ethical Safeguards

These are architectural, not policy-only.

  1. Local-first inference
  2. No raw perceptual retention
  3. User-visible opt-out at all times
  4. No silent mode changes
  5. No teacher-facing individual data
  6. Aggregate-only classroom views (optional)
  7. Explicit uncertainty disclosure

Calibration

Ægis improves by asking, not assuming. Occasional prompts — “Was that confusing?”, “Too fast / just right / too slow” — personalize thresholds, reinforce agency, and never train population-wide models without consent.
